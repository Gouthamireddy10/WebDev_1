Play Guide

  • Objective: Cricket is played between two teams of 11 players each. The aim is simple: score more runs than the opposition.
  • Teams: One team bats while the other fields. Matches are officiated by two umpires.
    • Types of Matches:
    • Test Match: The longest format, lasting up to five days. Each team bats twice.
    • One-Day Match: Lasts a day, with each team batting once (50 overs each).
    • Twenty20 (T20): The quickest format, with each team facing 20 overs.
  • Batting:Two batsmen at the crease. The bowler delivers the ball to the striker. Batsmen aim to score runs by hitting the ball and running between the wickets.When a batsman is out, another replaces them.
  • Bowling: The bowler aims to dismiss batsmen. Fielders position themselves to stop runs or take catches. Wicket-keeper stands behind the stumps.
    • Remember, cricket is not just about individual brilliance; it’s a team sport that combines physical fitness, mental acumen, and camaraderie.
